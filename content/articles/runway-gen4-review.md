When Runway dropped Gen-4, I expected incremental improvements. More seconds of generated video, slightly better consistency, a few new style options. What I got felt like watching a technology cross a threshold I hadn't realized existed.

The jump in visual fidelity is immediately apparent. Gen-3 had a certain smoothness to it—beautiful, but you could tell you were looking at AI. Gen-4 has texture. Grain. The subtle imperfections that make footage feel real. Light sources behave correctly. Motion blur happens naturally. Skin has pores and subtleties that no earlier model could manage.

Prompt following has improved dramatically. Describe a complex scene with multiple characters, specific actions, a particular mood, and precise camera movement. Gen-4 delivers something closer to what you imagined than what you hoped for. The gap between intention and output has shrunk significantly.

I've been using it for client work—short promotional content, social media material, concept visualization for film projects. The turnaround time is absurd compared to traditional production. A video that would have required a crew, equipment, and hours of shooting gets generated in minutes. For initial concepts and client approval, it's invaluable.

The new camera controls deserve their own discussion. You can now specify lens type, focal length, movement speed, and even sensor characteristics. This sounds technical, and it is, but Runway has made it accessible. The interface provides enough guidance that creatives without cinematography backgrounds can make informed choices. Results look professional instead of amateurish.

Motion consistency across clips has always been a challenge with AI video. Gen-4 handles character consistency much better—whether you're generating a single long clip or stitching together multiple shorter ones. Characters maintain their appearance, clothing, and movement patterns. Props behave correctly. It's not perfect (extreme angles and unusual poses still trip it up), but it's production-ready for most use cases.

One feature that's flown under the radar: the improved audio understanding. Gen-4 can now sync video output to specific audio tracks in ways that feel intuitive. Match the energy of your soundtrack, time cuts to beats, let the music drive the visual rhythm. It's a small addition that opens up creative possibilities.

For content creators specifically, Runway has become the default choice for a reason. The toolset beyond pure generation—motion tracking, inpainting, background removal—creates a workflow that doesn't require switching between multiple applications. It's a genuine production environment.

The main limitation remains duration. Even with improvements, you're still working with clips measured in seconds rather than minutes. Complex narratives require careful planning and multiple generations. But for the content it's designed for—short-form, high-impact visual material—Gen-4 delivers.

If you're creating any video content professionally and haven't integrated AI generation into your workflow, you're at a disadvantage. Runway Gen-4 is where I'd start.
